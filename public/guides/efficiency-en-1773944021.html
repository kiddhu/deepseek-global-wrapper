A training job, a batch inference run, and a real-time conversational agent have radically different compute profiles. Legacy clouds force developers to choose an instance type and size it for the worst-case scenario, leading to massive underutilization. You pay for the GPU's constant potential, not its intelligent application. * **The "Full Stack" Tax:** The cost of a legacy cloud GPU-hour isn't just for the silicon. It includes the amortized cost of globally redundant networks, vast sales organizations, undifferentiated storage services, and the immense overhead of maintaining universal compatibility. The developer building a focused, high-performance AI agent subsidizes this entire empire. * **Architectural Mismatch:** These platforms are designed for generality. The routing, scheduling, and data layers are optimized for serving millions of diverse web requests, not for the unique, data-intensive, model-parallel pathways required by modern large language models like DeepSeek R1. The result is latency overhead and resource contention that the developer cannot see or optimize. The consequence is a direct transfer of wealth from the innovator to the infrastructure landlord. A developer's burn rate becomes dominated by a cost factor that offers no competitive advantage, only the privilege of participation. This is the old world. ### **2.
