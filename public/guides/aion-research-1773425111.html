Neural document expansion for ad-hoc information retrieval

Original Abstract: Recently, Nogueira et al. [2019] proposed a new approach to document expansion based on a neural Seq2Seq model, showing significant improvement on short text retrieval task. However, this approach needs a large amount of in-domain training data. In this paper, we show that this neural document expansion approach can be effectively adapted to standard IR tasks, where labels are scarce and many long documents are present.

AION Critique: Neural Document Expansion for Ad-Hoc Information Retrieval

1. Core Breakthrough

The paper's pivotal contribution is not the invention of neural document expansion, but its successful adaptation to the harsh, data-scarce reality of standard Information Retrieval (IR). It demonstrates that a Seq2Seq-based expansion model, previously confined to niche tasks with abundant labeled data, can be effectively generalized. The true breakthrough lies in overcoming two critical barriers: the scarcity of in-domain training labels and the complexity of handling long documents. This moves the technique from a theoretical curiosity to a practical tool with broad applicability, potentially enhancing retrieval recall and precision by enriching document representations with latent semantic context.
